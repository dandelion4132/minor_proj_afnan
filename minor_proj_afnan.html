Historical Monuments of India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Construction started: 1631
  • Height: 73 m
  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahouri
  • Architectural style: Mughal architecture
  • Opened: 1648

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a minaret and "victory tower" located in Delhi, India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its elaborate decorations and inscriptions. The Qutub Minar was constructed by various rulers and reflects the Indo-Islamic architectural style.

  • Height: 73 m
  • Constructed by: Qutb al-Din Aibak, Iltutmish, Firuz Shah Tughlaq, Sher Shah Suri, Sikandar Lodi
  • Architectural style: Combination architecture

Arulmigu Ramanathaswamy Temple

Ramanathaswamy Temple (Rāmanātasvāmi Kōyil) is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Shiva located on Rameswaram island in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. It is also one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples. It is one of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams, where three of the most revered Nayanars (Saivite saints), Appar, Sundarar and Tirugnana Sambandar, have glorified the temple with their songs.

  • Architect: King Muthuramalinga Sethupathiy
  • Built: 17th century
  • Architectural style(s): Dravidian-style
  • Height: 30 feet

Charminar

The Charminar is a monument and mosque located in Hyderabad, India. It was constructed in 1591 and is known for its iconic four pillars, which give it its name. The Charminar is a recognized symbol of Hyderabad and showcases Islamic and Indo-Islamic architectural styles.

  • Height: 56 m
  • Opened: 1591
  • Architectural styles: combination
  • Materials: Granite, Limestone, Mortar, Marble

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in Delhi, India. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is known for its impressive architecture. The Red Fort was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan and showcases the Indo-Islamic and Mughal architectural styles.

  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahouri
  • Built: 12 May 1639 - 6 April 1648
  • Architectural style(s): Mughal
  • Designated as world heritage site: 2007
  • Height: 18–33 m

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it serves as a tribute to Indian soldiers who lost their lives in various wars. The India Gate is an architectural marvel and is often compared to other famous triumphal arches around the world.

  • Height: 42 m
  • Opened: 12 February 1931
